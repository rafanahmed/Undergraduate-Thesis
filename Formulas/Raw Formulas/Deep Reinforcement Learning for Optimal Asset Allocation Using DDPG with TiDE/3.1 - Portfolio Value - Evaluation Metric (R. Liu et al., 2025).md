Portfolio Value measures *total wealth accumulation* as a simple ratio of terminal to initial capital. It is the most intuitive performance metric and directly reflects the success of the compounding strategy.

$$
PV = \frac{W_{T}}{W_0}
$$

Where,

| $PV$   | Portfolio Value — the multiplicative factor by which initial wealth has grown. $PV = 2.0$ means the capital doubled. |
| ------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $W_T$  | Terminal wealth at time $T$ (see [[1.3 - Cumulative Wealth Transition (R. Liu et al., 2025)]]).                      |
| $W_0$  | Initial wealth at the start of the evaluation period.                                                                |

Portfolio Value is the **raw multiplicative outcome** of the investment strategy. While $LU$ is the quantity the agent *optimizes* (see [[3.0 - Logarithmic Utility - Evaluation Metric (R. Liu et al., 2025)]], $PV$ is the quantity investors ultimately *care about*. 

The relationship between them is $PV = e^{LU}$, confirming that maximizing log-utility is equivalent to maximizing portfolio value. $PV$ is reported alongside $LU$ because it provides immediate economic intuition: a $PV$ of 1.5 means a 50% total return, regardless of how many periods it took.

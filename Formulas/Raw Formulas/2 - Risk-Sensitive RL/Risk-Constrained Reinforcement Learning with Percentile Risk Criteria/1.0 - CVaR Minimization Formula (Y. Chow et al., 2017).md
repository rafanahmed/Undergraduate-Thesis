The **Conditional Value-at-Risk** (CVaR) at confidence level $\alpha \in (0, 1)$ quantifies the expected cost in the worst $(1-\alpha)$-fraction of outcomes. Unlike **Value-at-Risk** (VaR), which only reports a threshold, CVaR captures the *magnitude* of tail losses. The Rockafellar-Uryasev (2000) reformulation expresses CVaR as a minimization over an auxiliary parameter $\nu$, avoiding explicit quantile computation. The minimizer $\nu^*$ equals $\text{VaR}_\alpha(Z)$, and the auxiliary function $H_\alpha(Z, \nu) := \nu + \frac{1}{1-\alpha}\mathbb{E}[(Z - \nu)^+]$ is convex in $\nu$, making gradient-based joint optimization over policy parameters and $\nu$ tractable. This reformulation is the foundation for the entire risk-constrained optimization framework.

$$
\text{CVaR}_\alpha(Z) := \min_{\nu \in \mathbb{R}} \left\{\nu + \frac{1}{1 - \alpha}\mathbb{E}[(Z - \nu)^+]\right\}
$$

Where,

| $\text{CVaR}_\alpha(Z)$ | Conditional Value-at-Risk of the cost random variable $Z$ at confidence level $\alpha$. Equals the expected cost in the worst $(1-\alpha)$-fraction of outcomes. |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\alpha$                 | Confidence level, $\alpha \in (0, 1)$. Higher $\alpha$ means a smaller, more extreme tail is considered.                                                        |
| $Z$                      | Random variable representing cost, with $\mathbb{E}[\lvert Z \rvert] < \infty$. In the MDP context, $Z = J^\theta(x_0)$, the cumulative discounted constraint cost. |
| $\nu$                    | Auxiliary **VaR parameter**. The minimizer $\nu^*$ equals $\text{VaR}_\alpha(Z) = \min\{z \mid F_Z(z) \geq \alpha\}$.                                          |
| $(\cdot)^+$              | Positive part operator: $(x)^+ = \max(x, 0)$.                                                                                                                  |
| $\mathbb{E}[\cdot]$     | Expectation over the cost distribution induced by the policy $\mu(\cdot \mid x; \theta)$.                                                                       |

The CVaR reformulation converts a quantile-based risk measure into a smooth (almost everywhere) optimization problem. The function $H_\alpha(Z, \nu)$ is convex in $\nu$ and equals CVaR at its minimum. This means CVaR can be *jointly* optimized alongside the policy parameter $\theta$ by treating $\nu$ as an additional decision variable — the approach exploited by the CVaR-constrained MDP formulation (see [[1.1 - CVaR-Constrained MDP (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]]). The positive part $(Z - \nu)^+$ focuses attention exclusively on cost realizations that exceed the current VaR estimate $\nu$; outcomes below $\nu$ contribute zero to the objective. This tail-focus is both CVaR's strength (it captures *magnitude* of tail losses) and its computational weakness (gradient estimates depend on rare tail events).

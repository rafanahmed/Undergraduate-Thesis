The **policy gradient theorem** applied to the augmented MDP $\bar{M}$ provides the gradient of the Lagrangian with respect to $\theta$ in a form amenable to incremental (actor-critic) estimation. This is a direct application of the standard policy gradient theorem (Sutton et al., 2000; Peters et al., 2005) to the augmented state space $\bar{\mathcal{X}} = \mathcal{X} \times \mathcal{S}$, enabled by the augmented MDP cost identity (see [[3.0 - Augmented MDP Cost Identity (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]]) which establishes that $\nabla_\theta L(\nu, \theta, \lambda) = \nabla_\theta V^\theta(x_0, \nu)$.

$$
\nabla_\theta V^\theta(x_0, \nu) = \frac{1}{1 - \gamma}\sum_{x, s, a} \pi_\gamma^\theta(x, s, a \mid x_0, \nu)\,\nabla_\theta \log \mu(a \mid x, s;\, \theta)\,Q^\theta(x, s, a)
$$

Where,

| $\nabla_\theta V^\theta(x_0, \nu)$                   | Gradient of the augmented value function at the initial state $(x_0, \nu)$, equal to $\nabla_\theta L(\nu, \theta, \lambda)$.                     |
| ------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\gamma$                                               | Discount factor, $\gamma \in (0, 1)$.                                                                                                             |
| $\pi_\gamma^\theta(x, s, a \mid x_0, \nu)$           | $\gamma$-discounted occupation measure on $\bar{M}$: probability of visiting state-action $(x, s, a)$ under policy $\theta$ starting from $(x_0, \nu)$. |
| $\nabla_\theta \log \mu(a \mid x, s;\, \theta)$      | Score function for the augmented-state policy $\mu(a \mid x, s; \theta)$.                                                                         |
| $Q^\theta(x, s, a)$                                   | Action-value function on $\bar{M}$: expected discounted augmented cost from state $(x, s)$ after taking action $a$ and following $\theta$.        |
| $x, s, a$                                             | Augmented state components $x \in \mathcal{X}$, $s \in \mathcal{S}$, and action $a \in \mathcal{A}$.                                              |

This formula shows that the Lagrangian gradient can be estimated by collecting state-action-cost transitions from the augmented MDP, without requiring complete trajectories. The critic approximates $Q^\theta(x, s, a)$ (or equivalently $V^\theta(x, s)$) with linear function approximation $V^\theta(x, s) \approx v^\top \phi(x, s)$, and the TD error $\delta_k$ serves as an unbiased proxy for the advantage: $\frac{1}{1-\gamma}\nabla_\theta \log \mu(a_k \mid x_k, s_k; \theta) \cdot \delta_k$ is an unbiased estimator of $\nabla_\theta L(\nu, \theta, \lambda)$. The policy in the augmented MDP depends on *both* $x$ and $s$, so the policy must condition on the auxiliary budget state. This means the policy network must take the accumulated constraint cost as input — an architectural requirement imposed by the augmentation.

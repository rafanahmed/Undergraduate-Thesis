The gradient of the Lagrangian $L(\nu, \theta, \lambda)$ with respect to the policy parameter $\theta$ provides the descent direction for the policy update. It is derived by expanding expectations, applying the **likelihood-ratio trick** ($\nabla_\theta P_\theta(\xi) = P_\theta(\xi)\nabla_\theta \log P_\theta(\xi)$), and collecting terms. The result decomposes into two components: a standard policy gradient (minimize expected cost) and a *risk gradient* that is driven exclusively by the tail of the constraint cost distribution — only trajectories whose constraint cost $J(\xi)$ exceeds the VaR threshold $\nu$ contribute to the risk term.

$$
\nabla_\theta L = \sum_{\xi:\, P_\theta(\xi) \neq 0} P_\theta(\xi)\,\nabla_\theta \log P_\theta(\xi)\!\left(G(\xi) + \frac{\lambda}{1 - \alpha}(J(\xi) - \nu)\,\mathbf{1}\{J(\xi) \geq \nu\}\right)
$$

Where,

| $\nabla_\theta L$                 | Gradient of the Lagrangian with respect to the policy parameter $\theta$ (see [[2.0 - CVaR Lagrangian Function (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]]).                                              |
| ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $P_\theta(\xi)$                   | Probability of trajectory $\xi$ under policy $\theta$: $P_\theta(\xi) = P_0(x_0)\prod_{k=0}^{T-1}\mu(a_k \mid x_k; \theta)\,P(x_{k+1} \mid x_k, a_k)$.                              |
| $\nabla_\theta \log P_\theta(\xi)$ | Score function (likelihood ratio): $\sum_{k=0}^{T-1} \nabla_\theta \log \mu(a_k \mid x_k; \theta)$.                                                                                  |
| $G(\xi)$                          | Trajectory cost: $\sum_{k=0}^{T-1} \gamma^k C(x_k, a_k)$.                                                                                                                             |
| $\lambda$                          | Lagrange multiplier (see [[2.0 - CVaR Lagrangian Function (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]]).                                                                                                   |
| $\alpha$                           | CVaR confidence level.                                                                                                                                                                  |
| $J(\xi)$                          | Trajectory constraint cost: $\sum_{k=0}^{T-1} \gamma^k D(x_k, a_k)$.                                                                                                                  |
| $\nu$                              | VaR parameter (see [[1.0 - CVaR Minimization Formula (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]]).                                                                                                        |
| $\mathbf{1}\{J(\xi) \geq \nu\}$  | Indicator function: equals 1 when the trajectory's constraint cost exceeds $\nu$, and 0 otherwise.                                                                                      |

The first component, $\sum_\xi P_\theta(\xi)\nabla_\theta \log P_\theta(\xi) G(\xi)$, is the standard **REINFORCE** policy gradient for expected cost minimization. The second component weights the score function by the *excess* constraint cost $(J(\xi) - \nu)$ for tail trajectories only, scaled by $\lambda/(1-\alpha)$. The Lagrange multiplier $\lambda$ controls how strongly the risk gradient pulls the policy away from pure expected-cost descent. In the Monte Carlo estimation used by Algorithm 1, this gradient is approximated by averaging over $N$ sampled trajectories, yielding the $\theta$-update rule in the trajectory-based policy gradient algorithm.

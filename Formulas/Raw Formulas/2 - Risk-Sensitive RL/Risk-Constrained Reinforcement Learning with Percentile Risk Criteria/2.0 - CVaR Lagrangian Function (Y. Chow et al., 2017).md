The **Lagrangian relaxation** transforms the CVaR-constrained MDP (see [[1.1 - CVaR-Constrained MDP (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]]) into an unconstrained saddle-point problem. The Lagrange multiplier $\lambda$ penalizes constraint violations, converting the constrained optimization into a maximin problem that can be solved by simultaneously descending in $(\theta, \nu)$ and ascending in $\lambda$. The Lagrangian is linear in $\lambda$ and convex in $\nu$ (for fixed $\theta$), and the **saddle point theorem** (Bertsekas, 1999) guarantees that a local saddle point $(\nu^*, \theta^*, \lambda^*)$ yields a locally optimal policy $\theta^*$ for the original constrained problem. This convergence point merges the CVaR reformulation from the minimization formula (see [[1.0 - CVaR Minimization Formula (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]]) with the Lagrangian relaxation methodology.

$$
L(\nu, \theta, \lambda) := V^\theta(x_0) + \lambda\left(\nu + \frac{1}{1 - \alpha}\mathbb{E}\!\left[(J^\theta(x_0) - \nu)^+\right] - \beta\right)
$$

Where,

| $L(\nu, \theta, \lambda)$   | Lagrangian function for the CVaR-constrained MDP. The saddle-point problem is $\max_{\lambda \geq 0} \min_{\theta, \nu} L(\nu, \theta, \lambda)$. |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $V^\theta(x_0)$             | Expected cumulative discounted cost (see [[1.1 - CVaR-Constrained MDP (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]]).                                                 |
| $\lambda$                    | Lagrange multiplier, $\lambda \geq 0$. Controls how strongly constraint violations are penalized.                                                 |
| $\nu$                        | VaR parameter (see [[1.0 - CVaR Minimization Formula (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]]).                                                                  |
| $\alpha$                     | CVaR confidence level, $\alpha \in (0, 1)$.                                                                                                       |
| $J^\theta(x_0)$             | Cumulative discounted constraint cost (see [[1.1 - CVaR-Constrained MDP (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]]).                                               |
| $\beta$                      | Cost tolerance (risk budget).                                                                                                                      |
| $\theta$                     | Policy parameter vector.                                                                                                                           |
| $(J^\theta(x_0) - \nu)^+$   | Positive part: excess constraint cost above the VaR threshold $\nu$.                                                                               |

The Lagrangian serves a dual role in the convergence analysis: it is both the **objective** being optimized and the **Lyapunov function** for the stability proof. The **envelope theorem** (Milgrom and Segal, 2002) establishes that the derivative of the optimal Lagrangian value $L^*(\lambda) = L(\nu^*(\lambda), \theta^*(\lambda), \lambda)$ equals the partial derivative of $L$ w.r.t. $\lambda$ evaluated at the optimizers, ensuring that the $\lambda$-update performs true gradient ascent on $L^*$. The three gradients of $L$ — w.r.t. $\theta$, $\nu$, and $\lambda$ — are the descent/ascent directions used in both the policy gradient and actor-critic algorithms (see [[2.1 - Policy Gradient of Lagrangian (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]], [[2.2 - VaR Sub-gradient (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]], [[2.3 - Multiplier Gradient (Y. Chow et al., 2017)]]).

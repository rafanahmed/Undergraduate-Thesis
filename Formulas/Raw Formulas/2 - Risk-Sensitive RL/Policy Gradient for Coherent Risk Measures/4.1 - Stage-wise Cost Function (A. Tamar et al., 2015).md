The stage-wise cost function $h_\theta(x, a)$ is the risk-sensitive analogue of the action-value function $Q(x, a)$ that appears in the standard Policy Gradient Theorem. It encodes the immediate cost of action $a$ in state $x$ plus a correction term that accounts for how the policy gradient interacts with the risk envelope's adversarial density and KKT multipliers. This function is defined in Eq. 26 (Appendix E.4) and is the key quantity that multiplies the score function $\nabla_\theta \log \mu_\theta(a_t|x_t)$ in the Dynamic Policy Gradient Theorem (see [[4.1.1 - Dynamic Policy Gradient Theorem (A. Tamar et al., 2015)]]).

The function depends on the risk-sensitive value function $V_\theta$ from the Bellman equation (see [[4.0 - Risk-Sensitive Bellman Equation (A. Tamar et al., 2015)]]) and on the saddle-point solution $(\xi^*_{\theta,x}, \lambda^{*,P}_{\theta,x}, \lambda^{*,E}_{\theta,x}, \lambda^{*,I}_{\theta,x})$ of the state-dependent Lagrangian.

$$
h_\theta(x, a) = C(x, a) + \sum_{x' \in \mathcal{X}} P(x'|x, a)\,\xi^*_{\theta,x}(x')\!\left[\gamma V_\theta(x') - \lambda^{*,P}_{\theta,x} - \sum_{i \in \mathcal{I}} \lambda^{*,I}_{\theta,x}(i)\,\frac{df_i(\xi^*_{\theta,x},\, p)}{dp(x')} - \sum_{e \in \mathcal{E}} \lambda^{*,E}_{\theta,x}(e)\,\frac{dg_e(\xi^*_{\theta,x},\, p)}{dp(x')}\right]
$$

Where,

| $h_\theta(x, a)$                  | Stage-wise cost function for state-action pair $(x, a)$ under policy $\theta$.                                                                                                  |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $C(x, a)$                          | Immediate cost of taking action $a$ in state $x$.                                                                                                                               |
| $P(x'\|x, a)$                     | Transition probability from $(x, a)$ to next state $x'$.                                                                                                                        |
| $\xi^*_{\theta,x}(x')$            | Optimal adversarial density at state $x$, evaluated at next state $x'$. The worst-case density from the risk envelope at state $x$.                                             |
| $V_\theta(x')$                     | Risk-sensitive value function at next state $x'$ (see [[4.0 - Risk-Sensitive Bellman Equation (A. Tamar et al., 2015)]]).                                                        |
| $\gamma$                           | Discount factor.                                                                                                                                                                 |
| $\lambda^{*,P}_{\theta,x}$        | KKT multiplier for probability normalization at state $x$. Acts as a baseline.                                                                                                   |
| $\lambda^{*,I}_{\theta,x}(i)$     | KKT multipliers for inequality constraints $f_i$ at state $x$.                                                                                                                  |
| $df_i(\xi^*_{\theta,x}, p)/dp(x')$ | Derivative of inequality constraint $f_i$ with respect to the transition probability at $x'$, evaluated at the saddle point.                                                    |
| $\lambda^{*,E}_{\theta,x}(e)$     | KKT multipliers for equality constraints $g_e$ at state $x$.                                                                                                                    |
| $dg_e(\xi^*_{\theta,x}, p)/dp(x')$ | Derivative of equality constraint $g_e$ with respect to the transition probability at $x'$, evaluated at the saddle point.                                                      |

The stage-wise cost $h_\theta$ differs from the standard action-value function in two critical ways. First, the next-state value $V_\theta(x')$ is weighted by the **adversarial density** $\xi^*_{\theta,x}(x')$, reflecting the worst-case transition under the risk envelope. Second, the **KKT multiplier corrections** $\lambda^{*,P}_{\theta,x}$, $\lambda^{*,I}_{\theta,x}$, and $\lambda^{*,E}_{\theta,x}$ account for the sensitivity of the risk envelope constraints to changes in the transition probability induced by the policy gradient. In the risk-neutral case ($\xi^* = 1$, no constraints), $h_\theta$ reduces to the standard $Q$-function $Q_\theta(x, a) = C(x, a) + \gamma \sum_{x'} P(x'|x, a) V_\theta(x')$.

Because $h_\theta$ involves a sum over all next states $x'$, exact computation is intractable when $\mathcal{X}$ is large. The actor-critic algorithm addresses this via a **two-phase sampling procedure**: for each visited $(x, a)$, sample next states from $P(\cdot|x, a)$ and estimate $h_\theta$ empirically.

**Theorem 8** sharpens the asymptotic maximality result (see [[5.1.1 - Asymptotic Maximality (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]). For any nonterminating strategy $\Lambda$ (one where $V_s > 0$ for all $s$), the theorem establishes an exact equivalence between wealth-ratio convergence to zero and the divergence of cumulative worst-case expected log-growth advantages. Two strategies satisfying the right-hand condition are termed **essentially different under uncertainty**. The robust Kelly strategy dominates any essentially different strategy by magnitude almost surely.

$$
\left\{\lim_{N \to \infty} \frac{S_N}{S_N^*} = 0\right\} = \left\{\sum_{N=1}^\infty \bar{\mathbb{E}}[\log V_N^* - \log V_N \mid \mathcal{O}_{N-1}] = \infty\right\} \quad \text{a.s.}
$$

Where,

| $S_N / S_N^*$                            | Wealth ratio of strategy $\Lambda$ to the robust Kelly strategy (see [[5.1.1 - Asymptotic Maximality (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]). |
| ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $V_N^*$                                   | Period-$N$ growth factor under the robust Kelly strategy (see [[5.1 - Accumulated Wealth Growth (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).       |
| $V_N$                                     | Period-$N$ growth factor under the competing strategy $\Lambda$.                                                                |
| $\bar{\mathbb{E}}[\cdot \mid \mathcal{O}_{N-1}]$ | Non-linear conditional expectation (see [[5.0 - Non-Linear Expectation Operator (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).               |
| $\log V_N^* - \log V_N$                  | Per-period log-growth advantage of the robust Kelly bet over the competing bet.                                                  |
| $\sum_{N=1}^\infty$                      | Cumulative sum over all periods.                                                                                                 |
| $\mathcal{O}_{N-1}$                      | Observed outcomes during the first $N-1$ periods.                                                                                |

The proof combines the $G$-supermartingale structure from Theorem 7 with the **non-linear Doob martingale convergence theorem** (Peng, 2010; Soner, Touzi & Zhang, 2011). The sequence $\log(S_N^*/S_N) - \sum_{s=1}^N \bar{\mathbb{E}}[\log V_s^* - \log V_s \mid \mathcal{O}_{s-1}]$ forms a **$G$-martingale**, and convergence follows.
- The left-hand event — the alternative strategy's wealth becoming *infinitely smaller* than the robust Kelly wealth — occurs if and only if the cumulative worst-case expected log-growth advantage diverges.
- This is the strongest possible optimality statement: not just that the robust Kelly strategy performs as well as any alternative, but that it *infinitely dominates* any strategy with systematically different behavior.

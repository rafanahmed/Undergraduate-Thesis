**Theorem 7** of the paper proves that the distributional robust Kelly strategy asymptotically maximizes the worst-case growth rate. For any competing strategy $\Lambda$ with accumulated wealth $S_N$ (see [[5.1 - Accumulated Wealth Growth (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]), the ratio $S_N / S_N^*$ forms a **decreasing $G$-supermartingale** under the non-linear expectation operator (see [[5.0 - Non-Linear Expectation Operator (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]). This extends Breiman's (1961) classical result — which assumed a *fixed, known* distribution — to the setting where distributions vary adversarially within $\Xi$.

$$
\lim_N \frac{S_N}{S_N^*} \text{ exists a.s., and } \inf_{\pi_1, \dots, \pi_N \in \Xi} \mathbb{E}\left[\lim_N \frac{S_N}{S_N^*}\right] \leq 1
$$

Where,

| $S_N$                                   | Accumulated wealth of any competing strategy $\Lambda$ (see [[5.1 - Accumulated Wealth Growth (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).          |
| ---------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $S_N^*$                                  | Accumulated wealth of the distributional robust Kelly strategy $\Lambda^*$.                                                      |
| $S_N / S_N^*$                            | Wealth ratio — no strategy's wealth can systematically exceed the robust Kelly wealth in the worst case.                         |
| $\lim_N$                                 | Almost-sure limit as the number of periods $N \to \infty$.                                                                       |
| $\inf_{\pi_1, \dots, \pi_N \in \Xi}$    | Infimum over all possible sequences of distributions in $\Xi$.                                                                   |

The proof hinges on showing that for any period $N$ and any bet $\mathbf{b}_N$: $\bar{\mathbb{E}}[V_N / V_N^* \mid \mathcal{O}_{N-1}] \leq 1$. This is established by exploiting the **maximizing property** of the robust bet, the **superlinearity** of $\bar{\mathbb{E}}$ (see [[5.0 - Non-Linear Expectation Operator (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]), and **Fatou's lemma** for bounded sequences.
- The $G$-supermartingale property propagates from the single-period inequality to the accumulated wealth ratio.
- The practical implication: no strategy can systematically outgrow the robust Kelly strategy when nature selects distributions adversarially from $\Xi$.

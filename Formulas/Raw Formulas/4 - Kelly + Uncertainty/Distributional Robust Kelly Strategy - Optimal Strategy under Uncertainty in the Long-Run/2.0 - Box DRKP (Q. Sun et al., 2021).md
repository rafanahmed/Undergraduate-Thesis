The box uncertainty set is the most intuitive uncertainty model: each probability $\pi_k$ can deviate from its nominal value $\pi_{\text{nom},k}$ by at most $\rho_k$. This formulation (Theorem 2 in the paper) is the simplest concrete DCP form of the DRKP (see [[1.1.1 - Distributional Robust Kelly Problem (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]) and is used in the paper's horse racing numerical experiments. It is derived as a specialization of the general polyhedron case via Lagrangian duality, with the box constraints encoded as paired linear inequalities and simplified through the substitution $\boldsymbol{\mu} = \boldsymbol{\lambda}^+ - \boldsymbol{\lambda}^-$.

$$
\max_{\mathbf{b}, \boldsymbol{\mu}} \quad \min\left(\log(\mathbf{R}^\top \mathbf{b}) + \boldsymbol{\mu}\right) - \boldsymbol{\pi}_{\text{nom}}^\top \boldsymbol{\mu} - \boldsymbol{\rho}^\top |\boldsymbol{\mu}|
$$

Where,

| $\mathbf{b}$                            | Bet allocation vector (see [[1.0 - Mean Log Growth Rate (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).                                               |
| ---------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\boldsymbol{\mu}$                       | Dual variable vector $\in \mathbb{R}^K$, arising from Lagrangian duality over the box constraints.                              |
| $\min(\cdot)$                            | Minimum component of the vector argument.                                                                                        |
| $\log(\mathbf{R}^\top \mathbf{b})$      | Elementwise log of the wealth growth factors across all $K$ outcomes.                                                            |
| $\boldsymbol{\pi}_{\text{nom}}$          | Nominal probability distribution $\in \mathcal{S}_K$ — the best estimate of the true distribution.                              |
| $\boldsymbol{\rho}$                      | Vector of radii $\in \mathbb{R}^K_+$ controlling the maximum deviation of each probability from its nominal value.              |
| $\|\boldsymbol{\mu}\|$                   | Elementwise absolute value of $\boldsymbol{\mu}$.                                                                                |
| $\mathbf{R}$                             | Payoff matrix (see [[1.0 - Mean Log Growth Rate (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).                                                        |

The term $\boldsymbol{\rho}^\top |\boldsymbol{\mu}|$ acts as a **regularizer**: larger uncertainty radii penalize the dual variables more heavily, producing more conservative bets. When $\boldsymbol{\rho} = \mathbf{0}$, the problem reduces to the classic Kelly problem. The $\min(\cdot)$ of a concave vector expression is concave, and all other terms are linear or convex-composed, so the problem follows **DCP rules** and is directly implementable in CVXPY.
- In the horse racing experiment ($n = 20$ horses, $K = 190$ outcomes), the box DRKP with $\eta = 0.26$ turned a worst-case loss of $-2.2\%$ (classic Kelly) into a worst-case gain of $+0.7\%$ (robust Kelly), while sacrificing nominal growth from $4.3\%$ to $2.2\%$.
- The robust bet produces a more **diverse** allocation vector — spreading bets more evenly rather than concentrating on nominally favorable outcomes.

The non-linear expectation operator is the mathematical foundation for extending Breiman's classical Kelly optimality proofs to the distributional robust setting. It replaces the standard expectation $\mathbb{E}_\pi[\cdot]$ (which requires a known distribution) with the *worst-case* expectation over the uncertainty set $\Xi$. This operator, drawn from the non-linear expectation theory of Peng (2010), enables the definition of *$G$-supermartingales* and *$G$-martingales* that underpin the asymptotic optimality theorems (see [[5.1.1 - Asymptotic Maximality (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]] and [[5.1.1.1 - Magnitude Dominance (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).

$$
\bar{\mathbb{E}}[\cdot] := \inf_{\pi_N \in \Xi} \mathbb{E}_{\pi_N}[\cdot]
$$

Where,

| $\bar{\mathbb{E}}[\cdot]$              | Non-linear expectation operator — the worst-case expectation over the uncertainty set.                            |
| ---------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\inf_{\pi_N \in \Xi}$                  | Infimum over all distributions $\pi_N$ in the uncertainty set $\Xi$.                                              |
| $\mathbb{E}_{\pi_N}[\cdot]$             | Standard expectation under distribution $\pi_N$.                                                                  |
| $\Xi$                                    | Uncertainty set of plausible distributions (see [[1.1 - Worst-Case Log Growth Rate (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).      |

$\bar{\mathbb{E}}$ has three key properties:
- **Superlinear:** $\bar{\mathbb{E}}[X + Y] \geq \bar{\mathbb{E}}[X] + \bar{\mathbb{E}}[Y]$, because the infimum of a sum is at least the sum of infima.
- **Monotone:** $X \leq Y$ a.s. implies $\bar{\mathbb{E}}[X] \leq \bar{\mathbb{E}}[Y]$.
- **Constant-preserving:** $\bar{\mathbb{E}}[c] = c$ for constants $c$.

A sequence $\{M_N\}$ is a **$G$-supermartingale** under $\bar{\mathbb{E}}$ if $\bar{\mathbb{E}}[M_{N+1} \mid \mathcal{O}_N] \leq M_N$ a.s. for all $N$. The non-linear Doob convergence theorem then guarantees almost-sure convergence of such sequences, which is the key tool for proving asymptotic dominance of the robust Kelly strategy.

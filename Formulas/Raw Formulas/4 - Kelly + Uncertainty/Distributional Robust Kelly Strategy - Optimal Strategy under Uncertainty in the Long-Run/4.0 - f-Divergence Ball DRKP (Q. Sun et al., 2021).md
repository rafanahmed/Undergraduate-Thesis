The $f$-divergence ball defines the uncertainty set as all distributions within divergence $\epsilon$ of the nominal distribution $\boldsymbol{\pi}_{\text{nom}}$. The $f$-divergence is a general class of statistical distances parametrized by a convex function $f$ with $f(1) = 0$, encompassing KL-divergence, reverse KL, Pearson $\chi^2$, Neyman $\chi^2$, Hellinger distance, and total variation. This formulation (Theorem 4 in the paper) uses the **Fenchel conjugate** $f^*$ and its **perspective function** to obtain a DCP form of the DRKP (see [[1.1.1 - Distributional Robust Kelly Problem (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).

$$
\begin{aligned}
\max_{\mathbf{b},\, \lambda,\, \beta,\, \mathbf{w},\, \mathbf{z}} \quad & -\boldsymbol{\pi}_{\text{nom}}^\top \mathbf{w} - \epsilon\lambda - \beta \\
\text{s.t.} \quad & \mathbf{w} \geq \lambda\, f^*\!\left(\frac{\mathbf{z}}{\lambda}\right), \quad \mathbf{z} \geq -\log(\mathbf{R}^\top \mathbf{b}) - \beta, \quad \lambda \geq 0, \quad \mathbf{b} \in \mathcal{B}
\end{aligned}
$$

Where,

| $\mathbf{b}$                            | Bet allocation vector (see [[1.0 - Mean Log Growth Rate (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).                                                             |
| ---------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\lambda$                                | Dual variable $\in \mathbb{R}_+$ for the divergence radius constraint $D_f(\boldsymbol{\pi} \| \boldsymbol{\pi}_{\text{nom}}) \leq \epsilon$. |
| $\beta$                                  | Dual variable $\in \mathbb{R}$ for the simplex constraint $\mathbf{1}^\top \boldsymbol{\pi} = 1$.                                            |
| $\mathbf{w}$                             | Auxiliary variable $\in \mathbb{R}^K$ introduced by the convex relaxation.                                                                    |
| $\mathbf{z}$                             | Auxiliary variable $\in \mathbb{R}^K$ satisfying $\mathbf{z} \geq -\log(\mathbf{R}^\top \mathbf{b}) - \beta$.                                |
| $\boldsymbol{\pi}_{\text{nom}}$          | Nominal distribution (see [[2.0 - Box DRKP (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).                                                                         |
| $\epsilon$                               | Divergence radius — the maximum allowed $f$-divergence between $\boldsymbol{\pi}$ and $\boldsymbol{\pi}_{\text{nom}}$.                       |
| $f^*(s)$                                 | Fenchel conjugate of $f$: $f^*(s) = \sup_{t \geq 0}(ts - f(t))$.                                                                            |
| $\lambda f^*(\mathbf{z}/\lambda)$        | Perspective function of $f^*$, convex and non-decreasing in $\mathbf{z}$ when $f^*$ is convex and non-decreasing.                             |
| $\mathcal{B}$                            | Convex constraint set on bet allocation (see [[1.1.1 - Distributional Robust Kelly Problem (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).                          |

The $f$-divergence ball requires $\boldsymbol{\pi}$ to be **absolutely continuous** with respect to $\boldsymbol{\pi}_{\text{nom}}$ — it cannot assign positive probability to zero-probability outcomes. This limits its ability to model *black swan* events, unlike the Wasserstein set (see [[3.0 - Wasserstein Ball DRKP (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).
- DCP compliance relies on the **composition rule**: $-\log(\mathbf{R}^\top \mathbf{b}) - \beta$ is convex in $(\mathbf{b}, \beta)$, and the non-decreasing convex perspective function preserves convexity.
- Concrete examples of $f$: KL ($f(t) = t\log t - t + 1$, $f^*(s) = e^s - 1$), Pearson $\chi^2$ ($f(t) = \tfrac{1}{2}(t-1)^2$, $f^*(s) = \tfrac{1}{2}(s+1)^2 - \tfrac{1}{2}$), total variation ($f(t) = |t-1|$).

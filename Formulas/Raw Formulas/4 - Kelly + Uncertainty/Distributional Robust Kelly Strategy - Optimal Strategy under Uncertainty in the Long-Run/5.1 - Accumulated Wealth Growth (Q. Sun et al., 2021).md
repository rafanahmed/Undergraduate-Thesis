In the sequential gambling setting, a strategy $\Lambda = (\mathbf{b}_1, \dots, \mathbf{b}_N, \dots)$ generates wealth through multiplicative compounding. The accumulated wealth $S_N$ after $N$ periods is the product of all single-period growth factors. This quantity is the object whose long-run behavior is analyzed in the paper's asymptotic optimality results (see [[5.1.1 - Asymptotic Maximality (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]] and [[5.1.1.1 - Magnitude Dominance (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).

$$
S_0 = 1, \quad S_{N+1} = S_N \cdot V_N, \quad V_N := \mathbf{r}_N^\top \mathbf{b}_N
$$

Where,

| $S_N$                                   | Accumulated wealth growth after $N$ periods, starting from $S_0 = 1$.                                            |
| ---------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $V_N$                                    | Period-$N$ growth factor — the multiplicative factor by which wealth changes in period $N$.                       |
| $\mathbf{r}_N$                           | Random return vector at period $N$, with $r_{N,i} \in [0, R_M]$ (bounded returns).                               |
| $\mathbf{b}_N$                           | Bet allocation at period $N$ chosen by the strategy $\Lambda$.                                                    |
| $\mathbf{r}_N^\top \mathbf{b}_N$        | Wealth growth factor (see [[1.0 - Mean Log Growth Rate (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]); the dot product gives the total return. |

The recursive definition $S_{N+1} = S_N \cdot V_N$ encodes **multiplicative compounding**: $S_N = \prod_{s=0}^{N-1} V_s$. Taking logs, $\log S_N = \sum_{s=0}^{N-1} \log V_s$, which is a *random walk* with increments $\log V_s$.
- The distributional robust bet $\mathbf{b}_N^*$ maximizes $\inf_{\pi_N \in \Xi} \mathbb{E}_{\pi_N}[\log V_N \mid \mathcal{O}_{N-1}]$ — the worst-case expected log-growth per period (see [[5.0 - Non-Linear Expectation Operator (Q. Sun et al., 2021)]]).
- The distributions $\pi_N$ may vary freely across periods within $\Xi$, modeling an *adversarial* environment.

The paper generalizes the shrinkage factor beyond logarithmic utility to any risk-averse utility function $u$ with $u'' < 0$. By constructing the bootstrapped estimator $E(u^*(ks^*(Q)))$, expanding via Taylor series about $s^*(p)$, and maximizing with respect to $k$, a utility-agnostic rescaling formula is obtained. The formula does not depend explicitly on the form of $u$, but does so implicitly through the bet function $s^*(Q)$. This extends the scalar shrinkage methodology developed for logarithmic utility in [[2.0 - Shrinkage Factor Approximation (R. Baker et al., 2013)]] to the general case.

$$
k^* = \frac{s^*(p) \, E(s^*(Q))}{E(s^*(Q)^2)}
$$

Where,

| $k^*$          | Optimal shrinkage factor for a general risk-averse utility function.                                                                                                   |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $s^*(p)$       | Optimal bet at the true probability $p$, computed under the utility function $u$ (not necessarily the Kelly formula).                                                   |
| $s^*(Q)$       | Optimal bet at the random estimated probability $Q$, computed under the same utility function $u$.                                                                     |
| $E(s^*(Q))$    | Expected value of the optimal bet over the sampling distribution of $Q$.                                                                                               |
| $E(s^*(Q)^2)$  | Expected value of the squared optimal bet over the sampling distribution of $Q$: the second moment of $s^*(Q)$.                                                        |

The formula reveals that the shrinkage factor is the ratio of $s^*(p) \cdot E(s^*(Q))$ (a product of first moments) to $E(s^*(Q)^2)$ (a second moment). By the **Cauchy-Schwarz inequality**, $E(s^*(Q))^2 \leq E(s^*(Q)^2)$, and since $s^*(p) \leq E(s^*(Q))$ only when the bet function is concave (which is typical for risk-averse utilities), $k^* < 1$ in most cases. For logarithmic utility, this formula reduces to the [[2.0 - Shrinkage Factor Approximation (R. Baker et al., 2013)]]. The formula is valid for small variance $\sigma^2$ and provides the basis for extending the shrinkage methodology to any decision problem involving utility maximization under parameter uncertainty. The sufficient conditions under which $k^* < 1$ is guaranteed are established by the [[4.0 - Arrow-Pratt Shrinkage Condition (R. Baker et al., 2013)]].

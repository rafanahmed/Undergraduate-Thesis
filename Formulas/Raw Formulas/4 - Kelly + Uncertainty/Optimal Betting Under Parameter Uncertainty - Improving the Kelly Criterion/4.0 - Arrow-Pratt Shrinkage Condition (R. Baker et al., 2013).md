Theorem 3 of the paper establishes a sufficient condition for bet shrinkage under general risk-averse utility functions, expressed in terms of the **Arrow-Pratt measure of relative risk aversion**. This result identifies the class of utility functions and parameter regimes for which shrinkage is guaranteed, providing a theoretical boundary between the shrinkage and swelling regions. It complements the [[3.0 - General Rescaling Formula (R. Baker et al., 2013)]] by specifying *when* that formula's output will satisfy $k^* < 1$.

$$
R_r(1 + bs^*) < \frac{b^2}{b^2 - \frac{1}{1 - p}}
$$

Where,

| $R_r(x)$  | **Arrow-Pratt measure of relative risk aversion**: $R_r(x) = -xu''(x)/u'(x)$. Quantifies the curvature of the utility function relative to the wealth level.    |
| ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $b$        | Fractional odds.                                                                                                                                                 |
| $s^*$      | Optimal bet proportion at the true probability $p$, computed under the utility function $u$.                                                                     |
| $p$        | True winning probability.                                                                                                                                        |
| $u'(x)$    | First derivative of the utility function (marginal utility).                                                                                                     |
| $u''(x)$   | Second derivative of the utility function (measures concavity / risk aversion).                                                                                  |

The condition is automatically satisfied for **logarithmic utility** ($R_r = 1$) and **isoelastic utility** $u(x) = (x^\alpha - 1)/\alpha$ with $0 < \alpha < 1$ ($R_r = 1 - \alpha \leq 1$). It is *not* satisfied for **exponential utility** $u(x) = -\exp(-\lambda x)$, where $R_r = \lambda x$ can be arbitrarily large — this is the utility function that can produce **bet swelling** ($k^* > 1$) when odds are very favorable. The paper further establishes (Theorem 4) that if $u''' > 0$ and $p \leq (2b^2 + b - 1)/(b^3 + b^2 - b - 1)$, shrinkage is guaranteed for *any* smooth risk-averse utility. For $b \leq 2$ (even-money odds or worse), the probability bound is $p \leq 1$, meaning shrinkage is always required. This connects the paper's results to the broader economics literature on risk aversion, providing a classification of utility functions into those that always require shrinkage and those that may permit swelling.

To address the performance gap identified in the [[1.0 - Expected Maximized Utility Under Parameter Uncertainty (R. Baker et al., 2013)]], the paper introduces a **scaling factor** $k$ applied multiplicatively to the Kelly bet. Instead of wagering $s^*(Q)$, the bettor wagers $ks^*(Q)$, where $k \in [0, 1]$ is chosen to maximize expected out-of-sample utility. This formulation preserves the *shape* of the Kelly bet function while allowing a single scalar parameter to compress or expand all bet sizes uniformly.

$$
E(u^*) = \int_0^1 f(q) \left\{ p \ln(1 + bks^*(q)) + (1 - p) \ln(1 - ks^*(q)) \right\} dq
$$

Where,

| $E(u^*)$  | Expected maximized utility under the scaled bet $ks^*(q)$ (see [[1.0 - Expected Maximized Utility Under Parameter Uncertainty (R. Baker et al., 2013)]]).       |
| --------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $k$       | **Shrinkage factor**: a scalar multiplier applied to the Kelly bet. $k = 1$ recovers the raw Kelly bet; $k < 1$ shrinks it.                                    |
| $f(q)$    | Sampling distribution pdf of the estimated probability $Q$ (see [[1.0 - Expected Maximized Utility Under Parameter Uncertainty (R. Baker et al., 2013)]]).      |
| $p$       | True winning probability.                                                                                                                                       |
| $b$       | Fractional odds (see [[1.0 - Expected Maximized Utility Under Parameter Uncertainty (R. Baker et al., 2013)]]).                                                 |
| $s^*(q)$  | Kelly-optimal bet at estimated probability $q$ (see [[1.0 - Expected Maximized Utility Under Parameter Uncertainty (R. Baker et al., 2013)]]).                  |

The paper proves (Theorem 1) that $dE(u^*)/dk|_{k=1} < 0$ for any non-degenerate sampling distribution, which means the raw Kelly bet ($k = 1$) can *always* be improved by reducing $k$. Combined with $dE(u^*)/dk|_{k=0} > 0$ and the negative second derivative, there exists a unique optimal $k^* \in (0, 1)$. The optimization over $k$ transforms the intractable problem of finding the best bet function into a **one-dimensional search** over a scalar — the scaling factor.

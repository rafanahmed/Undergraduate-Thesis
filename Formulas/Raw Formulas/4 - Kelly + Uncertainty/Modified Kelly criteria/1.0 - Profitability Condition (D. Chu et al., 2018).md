The profitability condition defines the minimum winning probability required for a gambling system to have a positive expected return under European odds. It is the *threshold* below which no amount of money management can overcome the bookmaker's **vigorish**. Every formula in the modified Kelly framework presupposes that this condition holds.

$$
p > \frac{1}{\theta}
$$

Where,

| $p$      | True probability of placing a winning wager under the gambler's system.                                                              |
| -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| $\theta$ | **European (decimal) odds**: a winning bet of $\$x$ returns $x\theta$. Corresponds to American odds via standard conversion.         |

The profitability condition is the **entry gate** for the entire Kelly framework. When $p \leq 1/\theta$, the expected return per wager is non-positive, and the Kelly criterion prescribes $f = 0$ (do not bet). For $\theta = 1.909$ (American $-110$, the industry standard), the threshold is $p > 0.524$. This condition is *deceptively low* — while 52.4% seems achievable, bookmakers have survived for centuries because consistently exceeding this threshold is extremely difficult. The condition also encodes the vigorish: lower $\theta$ (higher vigorish) raises the profitability bar, compressing the margin available for profitable wagering.

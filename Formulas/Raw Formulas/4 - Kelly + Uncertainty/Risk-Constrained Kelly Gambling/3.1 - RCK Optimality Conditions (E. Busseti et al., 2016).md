The optimality conditions for the Risk-Constrained Kelly problem (see [[3.0 - Risk-Constrained Kelly Gambling Problem (E. Busseti et al., 2016)]]) are necessary and sufficient KKT conditions derived via directional derivatives of the Lagrangian. They provide a verifiable certificate of optimality: given a candidate solution $(\mathbf{b}^*, \kappa^*)$, these conditions can be checked using Monte Carlo estimation of the expectations. The conditions encode the economic principle that at optimality, every active bet has the same marginal benefit (growth contribution plus risk-penalty-weighted risk contribution).

$$
\begin{aligned}
&\mathbf{1}^\top \mathbf{b}^* = 1, \quad \mathbf{b}^* \geq 0, \quad E[(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b}^*)^{-\lambda}] \leq 1 \\
&\kappa^* \geq 0, \quad \kappa^*(E[(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b}^*)^{-\lambda}] - 1) = 0 \\
&E\left[\frac{r_i}{\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b}^*}\right] + \kappa^* \lambda E\left[\frac{r_i}{(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b}^*)^{\lambda+1}}\right] \begin{cases} \leq 1 + \kappa^* \lambda & \text{if } b_i^* = 0 \\ = 1 + \kappa^* \lambda & \text{if } b_i^* > 0 \end{cases}
\end{aligned}
$$

Where,

| $\mathbf{b}^*$                                                        | Optimal bet vector solving the RCK problem (see [[3.0 - Risk-Constrained Kelly Gambling Problem (E. Busseti et al., 2016)]]).                         |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\kappa^*$                                                             | Optimal dual variable (Lagrange multiplier) for the drawdown risk constraint. Existence guaranteed by Lemma 2 (Slater's condition).                   |
| $E[r_i / (\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b}^*)]$                            | Marginal contribution of bet $i$ to the growth rate at the optimum.                                                                                   |
| $E[r_i / (\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b}^*)^{\lambda+1}]$                | Marginal contribution of bet $i$ to the drawdown risk constraint at the optimum.                                                                      |
| $\lambda$                                                              | Risk-aversion parameter (see [[2.0 - Drawdown Risk Bound (E. Busseti et al., 2016)]]).                                                               |
| $\kappa^*(E[(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b}^*)^{-\lambda}] - 1) = 0$    | Complementary slackness: either the dual variable is zero or the risk constraint is active.                                                           |

The third condition implements **complementary slackness** at the bet level: for active bets ($b_i^* > 0$), the marginal growth benefit $E[r_i / (\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b}^*)]$ plus the risk-penalty-weighted marginal risk $\kappa^* \lambda E[r_i / (\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b}^*)^{\lambda+1}]$ equals the constant $1 + \kappa^* \lambda$. For inactive bets ($b_i^* = 0$), this sum is weakly less ‚Äî the bet is not worth activating. The conditions are derived in Lemma 4 of the paper's appendix via the right-sided derivative of $\varphi(\varepsilon) = -E[\log(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b}_\varepsilon)] + \kappa^* E[(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b}_\varepsilon)^{-\lambda}]$ along feasible directions $\mathbf{b}_\varepsilon = (1-\varepsilon)\mathbf{b}^* + \varepsilon \mathbf{b}$.

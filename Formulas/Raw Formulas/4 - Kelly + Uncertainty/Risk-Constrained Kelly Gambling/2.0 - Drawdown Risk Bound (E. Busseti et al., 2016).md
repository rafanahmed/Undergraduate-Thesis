The drawdown risk bound is the paper's central analytical innovation. It converts the intractable path-dependent constraint $\Pr(W_{\min} < \alpha) < \beta$ into a tractable *one-step* moment condition on the bet vector $\mathbf{b}$. The bound is derived via a **tilted probability measure** argument (Lemma 5 of the paper), which applies a modified Wald identity from sequential analysis to the log-wealth random walk. The condition $E[(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b})^{-\lambda}] \leq 1$ ensures that the moment generating function of the negative log-return at parameter $\lambda$ is bounded, which via the tilted measure translates into a bound on the probability that the wealth trajectory ever crosses the threshold $\alpha$.

$$
E[(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b})^{-\lambda}] \leq 1 \implies \Pr(W_{\min} < \alpha) < \beta
$$

Where,

| $E[\cdot]$                                     | Expectation over the return distribution.                                                                                                               |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b})^{-\lambda}$     | The gross portfolio return raised to the negative $\lambda$ power. A convex function of $\mathbf{b}$ for $\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b} > 0$.             |
| $\lambda$                                       | Risk-aversion parameter, defined as $\lambda = \log \beta / \log \alpha$. Controls the trade-off between growth rate and drawdown risk.                |
| $W_{\min}$                                      | Minimum wealth: $W_{\min} = \inf_{t \geq 1} w_t$, the infimum of the wealth trajectory over all epochs.                                               |
| $\alpha$                                        | Drawdown threshold $\alpha \in (0, 1)$. The undesired minimum wealth level (e.g., $\alpha = 0.7$ means a 30% drawdown).                                |
| $\beta$                                         | Risk tolerance $\beta \in (0, 1)$. The maximum acceptable probability of wealth dropping below $\alpha$ (e.g., $\beta = 0.1$ means 10% probability).  |
| $\Pr(W_{\min} < \alpha)$                        | Drawdown risk: the probability that the wealth trajectory ever falls below $\alpha$.                                                                    |

The bound is **convex** in $\mathbf{b}$: the function $x^{-\lambda}$ is convex for $x > 0$ and $\lambda > 0$, and convexity is preserved under expectation. This convexity is what makes the bound computationally useful — it can be embedded as a constraint in a convex optimization problem (see [[3.0 - Risk-Constrained Kelly Gambling Problem (E. Busseti et al., 2016)]]). The bound is *conservative*: Monte Carlo simulations show actual drawdown risk is typically 30% below the theoretical bound. For a fixed $\lambda$, the bound constrains the entire CDF of $W_{\min}$: $\Pr(W_{\min} < \alpha) < \alpha^\lambda$ for all $\alpha \in (0, 1)$. As $\lambda \to 0$, the bound becomes vacuous; as $\lambda \to \infty$, it forces $\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b} \geq 1$ almost surely (only risk-free bets).

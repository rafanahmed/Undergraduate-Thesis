The Risk-Constrained Kelly (RCK) gambling problem augments the Kelly gambling problem (see [[1.1 - Kelly Gambling Problem (E. Busseti et al., 2016)]]) with the drawdown risk bound (see [[2.0 - Drawdown Risk Bound (E. Busseti et al., 2016)]]) as an additional constraint. It is a *restriction* of the ideal drawdown-constrained problem — its feasible set is smaller, meaning any RCK-feasible bet is guaranteed to satisfy the drawdown constraint $\Pr(W_{\min} < \alpha) < \beta$. The problem is parametrized by a single risk-aversion parameter $\lambda = \log \beta / \log \alpha$ that controls the trade-off between growth rate and drawdown risk.

$$
\begin{aligned}
&\text{maximize} \quad E[\log(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b})] \\
&\text{subject to} \quad \mathbf{1}^\top \mathbf{b} = 1, \quad \mathbf{b} \geq 0, \\
&\phantom{\text{subject to} \quad} E[(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b})^{-\lambda}] \leq 1
\end{aligned}
$$

Where,

| $E[\log(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b})]$                    | [[1.0 - Expected Growth Rate (E. Busseti et al., 2016)]]. The objective to be maximized.                                                               |
| --------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\mathbf{b}$                                              | Decision variable: the bet allocation vector $\mathbf{b} \in \mathbb{R}^n$.                                                                            |
| $\mathbf{1}^\top \mathbf{b} = 1$                         | Budget constraint: all wealth must be allocated.                                                                                                        |
| $\mathbf{b} \geq 0$                                      | Long-only constraint.                                                                                                                                   |
| $E[(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b})^{-\lambda}] \leq 1$     | [[2.0 - Drawdown Risk Bound (E. Busseti et al., 2016)]]. Ensures $\Pr(W_{\min} < \alpha) < \beta$.                                                    |
| $\lambda$                                                 | Risk-aversion parameter: $\lambda = \log \beta / \log \alpha$. As $\lambda \to 0$, RCK reduces to unconstrained Kelly; as $\lambda \to \infty$, only risk-free bets are feasible. |

The RCK problem is **convex**: the objective $E[\log(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b})]$ is concave, and the constraint $E[(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b})^{-\lambda}] \leq 1$ is a sublevel set of a convex function. The trivial bet $\mathbf{b} = \mathbf{e}_n$ is always feasible. For finite outcomes, the problem is reformulated via log-sum-exp and solved with DCP-compliant solvers. For general returns, a **primal-dual stochastic gradient method** applied to the Lagrangian $L(\mathbf{b}, \kappa) = -E[\log(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b})] + \kappa(E[(\mathbf{r}^\top \mathbf{b})^{-\lambda}] - 1)$ converges to the optimum. The RCK solution strictly dominates fractional Kelly bets on the growth-risk frontier — it achieves higher growth rates for the same drawdown risk level.

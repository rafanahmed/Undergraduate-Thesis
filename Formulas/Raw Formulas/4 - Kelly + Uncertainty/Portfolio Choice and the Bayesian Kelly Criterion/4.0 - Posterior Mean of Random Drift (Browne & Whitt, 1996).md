The continuous-time **Bayesian filtering** result for an unobserved constant drift $Z$ in a diffusion process. When the observed process satisfies $dX_t = Z \, dt + \sigma \, dW_t$ and the prior on $Z$ is Gaussian, $Z \sim N(\mu, c)$, the posterior distribution of $Z$ given the observation filtration $\mathcal{F}_t^X$ remains Gaussian for all $t$. The posterior mean is a *precision-weighted combination* of the prior mean and the observation-based estimate, and the posterior variance decreases deterministically in time. This is a special case of the **Kalman filter** and provides the filtered drift that enters the **Optimal Bayesian Gambling Control** (see [[5.0 - Optimal Bayesian Gambling Control (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]).

$$
Z \mid \mathcal{F}_t^X \sim N\!\left(\frac{cX_t + \mu\sigma^2}{\sigma^2 + ct}, \; \frac{\sigma^2 c}{\sigma^2 + ct}\right)
$$

Where,

| $Z$                                                     | Unobserved constant drift — a random variable drawn once from the prior, fixed thereafter.                      |
| -------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\mathcal{F}_t^X$                                       | Observation filtration — the information available to the investor from observing $X_s$ for $s \in [0, t]$.     |
| $X_t$                                                    | Observed diffusion process: $dX_t = Z \, dt + \sigma \, dW_t$.                                                 |
| $\mu$                                                    | Prior mean of $Z$.                                                                                               |
| $c$                                                      | Prior variance of $Z$.                                                                                           |
| $\sigma^2$                                               | Known variance rate of the observation noise.                                                                    |
| $\frac{cX_t + \mu\sigma^2}{\sigma^2 + ct}$             | Posterior mean of $Z$: weighted combination of the data-driven estimate $X_t/t$ and the prior mean $\mu$.       |
| $\frac{\sigma^2 c}{\sigma^2 + ct}$                     | Posterior variance: decreases deterministically at rate $O(1/t)$, independent of the observed path.              |

The posterior mean $\hat{Z}_t = (cX_t + \mu\sigma^2)/(\sigma^2 + ct)$ can be decomposed as a weighted average: $\hat{Z}_t = w_t \cdot (X_t/t) + (1 - w_t) \cdot \mu$, where the data weight $w_t = ct/(\sigma^2 + ct)$ increases from $0$ to $1$ as $t$ grows. At $t = 0$, the investor relies entirely on the prior; as $t \to \infty$, the investor relies entirely on the data. The **path-independent** decay of the posterior variance is a special property of the Gaussian case — it means the *rate* of learning depends only on the noise level $\sigma^2$ and the prior uncertainty $c$, not on the actual trajectory of $X_t$. This filtering result feeds directly into the **Filtered Diffusion SDE** (see [[4.1 - Filtered Diffusion SDE (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]).

The **conjugate Bayesian updating** mechanism for the unknown success probability $\theta$ in the discrete-time gambling model. When the prior on $\theta$ is a **Beta distribution** with parameters $\alpha$ and $\beta$, the posterior after observing $n$ rounds with $Y_n$ total wins remains in the Beta family â€” a consequence of Beta-Binomial conjugacy. This closed-form updating is the engine of the paper's discrete-time Bayesian framework and the basis for the **Discrete Bayesian Kelly Rule** (see [[3.1 - Discrete Bayesian Kelly Rule (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]).

$$
\begin{aligned}
\theta &\sim \text{Beta}(\alpha, \beta) \\
\theta \mid Y_n = y &\sim \text{Beta}\!\left(\alpha + \frac{n+y}{2}, \; \beta + \frac{n-y}{2}\right) \\
E(\theta \mid Y_n) &= \frac{Y_n + n + 2\alpha}{2(n + \alpha + \beta)}
\end{aligned}
$$

Where,

| $\theta$                  | Unknown success probability â€” the probability that $Z_n = +1$ at each round.                                   |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\alpha$                  | First Beta prior parameter. Interpretable as the number of prior "pseudo-wins."                                  |
| $\beta$                   | Second Beta prior parameter. Interpretable as the number of prior "pseudo-losses."                               |
| $Y_n$                     | Sufficient statistic: total number of wins in $n$ rounds, $Y_n = \sum_{i=1}^n W_i$ where $W_i = (Z_i + 1)/2$. |
| $n$                       | Number of rounds observed.                                                                                       |
| $E(\theta \mid Y_n)$     | Posterior mean of $\theta$ â€” the precision-weighted average of the prior mean and the sample success rate.       |

The posterior mean $E(\theta \mid Y_n) = (Y_n + n + 2\alpha) / [2(n + \alpha + \beta)]$ is a **shrinkage estimator**: it pulls the sample success rate $Y_n/n$ toward the prior mean $\alpha/(\alpha + \beta)$, with the degree of shrinkage determined by the prior strength $\alpha + \beta$ relative to the sample size $n$. As $n \to \infty$, the posterior concentrates on the true $\theta$ and the prior's influence vanishes. The posterior variance $V(\theta \mid Y_n) = (Y_n + n + 2\alpha)(n - Y_n + 2\beta) / [4(n + \alpha + \beta)^2(n + \alpha + \beta + 1)]$ decreases at rate $O(1/n)$, consistent with the Bernstein-von Mises theorem. This Beta posterior is also used in [[PNOTES - Modified Kelly criteria]] and [[PNOTES - Optimal Betting Under Parameter Uncertainty - Improving the Kelly Criterion]] as the standard Bayesian model for binary outcome probabilities.

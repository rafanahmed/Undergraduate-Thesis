The **innovations representation** of the observed diffusion process after Bayesian filtering. The Fujisaki-Kallianpur-Kunita (FKK) theorem converts the partially observed process $dX_t = Z \, dt + \sigma \, dW_t$ (with $Z$ unobserved) into a fully observed SDE driven by the **innovations Brownian motion** $W_t'$. The unknown true drift $Z$ is replaced by the **posterior mean** $\hat{Z}_t = (cX_t + \mu\sigma^2)/(\sigma^2 + ct)$ (see [[4.0 - Posterior Mean of Random Drift (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]). This transformation is the technical key to solving the Bayesian control problem — it converts a partially observed stochastic control problem into a standard fully observed one.

$$
dX_t = \frac{cX_t + \mu\sigma^2}{\sigma^2 + ct} \, dt + \sigma \, dW_t'
$$

Where,

| $X_t$                                                   | Observed diffusion process — the cumulative return or position process.                                          |
| -------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\frac{cX_t + \mu\sigma^2}{\sigma^2 + ct}$             | Posterior mean of $Z$ given $\mathcal{F}_t^X$ (see [[4.0 - Posterior Mean of Random Drift (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]). Serves as the *filtered drift*. |
| $c$                                                      | Prior variance of the unobserved drift $Z$.                                                                      |
| $\mu$                                                    | Prior mean of $Z$.                                                                                               |
| $\sigma^2$                                               | Known variance rate of the observation noise.                                                                    |
| $\sigma$                                                 | Diffusion coefficient (volatility).                                                                              |
| $W_t'$                                                   | **Innovations Brownian motion** — a standard Brownian motion with respect to the observation filtration $\mathcal{F}_t^X$. |

The innovations process $W_t'$ is *different* from the original Brownian motion $W_t$: it is the residual noise after subtracting the best estimate of the drift. The filtered SDE has **time-varying, state-dependent drift** — at each moment, the drift coefficient depends on the current position $X_t$ (through the posterior mean) and on time $t$ (through the decreasing posterior variance). The explicit solution is $X_t = \mu t + (\sigma^2 + ct)[X_0 + \int_0^t \sigma/(\sigma^2 + cs) \, dW_s]$. This SDE is the *input* to the wealth control problem: wealth satisfies $dV_t = f_t V_t \, dX_t$, and the **Optimal Bayesian Gambling Control** (see [[5.0 - Optimal Bayesian Gambling Control (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]) is derived by maximizing expected log terminal wealth with respect to $f_t$ given this filtered dynamic.

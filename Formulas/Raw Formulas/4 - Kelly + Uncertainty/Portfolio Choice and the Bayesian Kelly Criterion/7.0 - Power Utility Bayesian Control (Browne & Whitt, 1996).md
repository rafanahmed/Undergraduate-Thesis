The Bayesian optimal betting fraction for **power utility** $u(x) = x^\eta$ ($\eta < 1$, $\eta \neq 0$), which demonstrates the **failure of certainty equivalence** outside the logarithmic case. Unlike the log-utility Bayesian Kelly rule (see [[5.0 - Optimal Bayesian Gambling Control (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]) where the optimal control depends only on the current posterior mean, the power-utility control depends on a **nonlinear recursion** $g_n$ that encodes the entire future evolution of the posterior distribution. The investor must hedge against the *volatility of the posterior estimate itself*, not just the volatility of returns.

$$
f^*(x, S_k, k) = \frac{1}{\Delta} \cdot \frac{[\theta_k(S_k) g_{n-k}(k+1, S_k+1)]^{1/(1-\eta)} - [(1-\theta_k(S_k)) g_{n-k}(k+1, S_k-1)]^{1/(1-\eta)}}{[\theta_k(S_k) g_{n-k}(k+1, S_k+1)]^{1/(1-\eta)} + [(1-\theta_k(S_k)) g_{n-k}(k+1, S_k-1)]^{1/(1-\eta)}}
$$

Where,

| $f^*(x, S_k, k)$                  | Optimal bet fraction at step $k$ with $n - k$ steps remaining, given walk position $S_k$.                      |
| ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\Delta$                           | Step size of the discrete game increments: $Z_n \in \{\pm \Delta\}$.                                           |
| $\theta_k(S_k)$                   | Posterior mean of $\theta$ given the walk position $S_k$ and $k$ observations (see [[3.0 - Beta Prior and Posterior Update (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]). |
| $g_{n-k}(k+1, S_k \pm 1)$        | Auxiliary value function at future states — encodes the remaining value of the game if the next step is a win ($+1$) or loss ($-1$). |
| $\eta$                             | Power utility exponent: $u(x) = x^\eta$. Log utility is the limiting case $\eta \to 0$.                        |
| $1/(1-\eta)$                       | Risk-aversion scaling exponent — controls how the curvature of utility distorts the optimal bet relative to the Kelly fraction. |

The auxiliary function $g_n(r, v)$ satisfies a **nonlinear recursion**: $g_n(r, v) = [\theta_r(v) g_{n-1}(r+1, v+1)^\eta + (1-\theta_r(v)) g_{n-1}(r+1, v-1)^\eta]^{1/\eta}$, with $g_0 = 1$. This recursion makes the optimal control **horizon-dependent** and **path-dependent** through the value of $g_n$ at future posterior states. The key structural difference from the log-utility case is that $g_n$ does *not* factor out of the optimization: the nonlinearity of power utility introduces an interaction between the curvature of the value function and the dispersion of the posterior. As $\eta \to 0$, the recursion linearizes and recovers the myopic certainty-equivalent rule of the **Discrete Bayesian Kelly Rule** (see [[3.1 - Discrete Bayesian Kelly Rule (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]). For $\eta < 0$ (more risk-averse than log), the optimal bet is **smaller** than the certainty equivalent would suggest — the investor *under-bets* relative to the posterior mean to hedge against estimation risk. This is the theoretical basis for the **bet shrinkage** phenomenon documented in [[PNOTES - Optimal Betting Under Parameter Uncertainty - Improving the Kelly Criterion]].

The fundamental wealth process underlying all Kelly-type gambling and investment models. At each discrete time step $n$, the gambler wagers a fraction $f_n$ of current wealth $V_{n-1}$ on a game with binary payoff increment $Z_n \in \{+1, -1\}$. Wealth evolves *multiplicatively* — each bet scales the entire capital by a factor that depends on the outcome. This multiplicative structure is the reason **logarithmic utility** is the natural objective for long-run growth: taking logs converts the product into a sum, making the Law of Large Numbers directly applicable.

$$
V_n = V_{n-1} + f_n V_{n-1} Z_n = V_0 \prod_{i=1}^n (1 + f_i Z_i)
$$

Where,

| $V_n$   | Wealth after the $n$-th bet.                                                                                     |
| ------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $V_0$   | Initial wealth at time zero.                                                                                     |
| $f_n$   | Fraction of wealth wagered at step $n$, with $0 \le f_n \le 1$ in the unconstrained case.                       |
| $Z_n$   | Payoff increment at step $n$: $Z_n = +1$ (win) with probability $\theta$, $Z_n = -1$ (loss) with probability $1 - \theta$. |
| $\prod$ | Product operator, reflecting the multiplicative compounding of returns.                                          |

The multiplicative form $V_n = V_0 \prod (1 + f_i Z_i)$ makes clear that a single large loss ($f_i Z_i = -1$, i.e., $f_i = 1$ and a loss) results in **total ruin** — wealth goes to zero and never recovers. This *absorbing barrier* at zero is the fundamental constraint that prevents the gambler from ever wagering their entire stake. The Kelly criterion emerges as the strategy that maximizes the expected growth rate $E[\ln(V_n/V_0)] / n$ subject to this multiplicative dynamic. The wealth process is the root dependency for all subsequent formulas in the paper, providing the dynamic that the **Classical Kelly Fraction** (see [[1.1 - Classical Kelly Fraction (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]) optimizes and that the **Continuous-Time Wealth SDE** (see [[2.0 - Continuous-Time Wealth SDE (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]) extends to diffusion form.

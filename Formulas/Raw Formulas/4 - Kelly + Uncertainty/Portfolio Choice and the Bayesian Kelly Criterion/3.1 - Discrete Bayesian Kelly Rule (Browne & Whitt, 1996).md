The optimal betting fraction in discrete time when the success probability $\theta$ is unknown and estimated via **Bayesian updating** from a Beta prior (see [[3.0 - Beta Prior and Posterior Update (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]). This formula is the **certainty equivalent** of the classical **Kelly fraction** (see [[1.1 - Classical Kelly Fraction (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]): the unknown $\theta$ is replaced by its posterior mean $E(\theta \mid S_j, j)$, and the standard Kelly rule $f^* = (2\theta - 1)/\Delta$ is applied as if the posterior mean were the truth. Under logarithmic utility, this myopic policy is provably globally optimal — it achieves the maximum expected log terminal wealth across all admissible strategies.

$$
f_{j+1}^*(S_j, j) = \frac{1}{\Delta} \cdot \frac{S_j + \alpha - \beta}{j + \alpha + \beta}
$$

Where,

| $f_{j+1}^*(S_j, j)$ | Optimal bet fraction at step $j+1$, conditioned on the walk position $S_j$ and the number of prior steps $j$. |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\Delta$              | Step size of the discrete game increments: $Z_n \in \{\pm \Delta\}$.                                           |
| $S_j$                 | Random walk position at step $j$: $S_j = \sum_{i=1}^j Z_i$, the cumulative net payoff.                        |
| $\alpha$              | First Beta prior parameter (see [[3.0 - Beta Prior and Posterior Update (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]).              |
| $\beta$               | Second Beta prior parameter (see [[3.0 - Beta Prior and Posterior Update (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]).             |
| $j$                   | Number of rounds already observed.                                                                               |

The numerator $S_j + \alpha - \beta$ captures the *net evidence* for the game being favorable: $S_j$ is the observed net gain from $j$ rounds, and $\alpha - \beta$ is the prior's net belief. The denominator $j + \alpha + \beta$ is the total precision — sample size plus prior strength. The fraction $(S_j + \alpha - \beta)/(j + \alpha + \beta) = 2E(\theta \mid S_j, j) - 1$ is the posterior mean of the excess success probability $2\theta - 1$, confirming the **certainty equivalence** structure. This rule is **state-dependent** — it adapts the bet size based on accumulated observations — and converges to the true Kelly fraction as $j \to \infty$. In the diffusion limit, it converges to the continuous-time **Optimal Bayesian Gambling Control** (see [[5.0 - Optimal Bayesian Gambling Control (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]).

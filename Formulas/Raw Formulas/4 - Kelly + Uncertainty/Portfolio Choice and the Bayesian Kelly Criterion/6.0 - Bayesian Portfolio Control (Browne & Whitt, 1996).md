The Bayesian extension of **Merton's log-optimal portfolio rule** to the case where the stock's drift is unobserved. When a risk-free asset earning rate $r$ is available alongside the risky asset, the optimal allocation to the risky asset under logarithmic utility and Bayesian updating replaces the unknown excess return $Z - r$ with its posterior estimate $\hat{Z}_t - r$. For a general prior $G$ on the drift, the formula reduces to the **posterior mean minus the risk-free rate**, divided by the variance — the cleanest distillation of the paper's central message.

$$
\begin{aligned}
f_t^* &= \frac{1}{\sigma^2}\left(\frac{cX_t + \mu\sigma^2}{\sigma^2 + ct} - r\right) \\[6pt]
f_t^* &= \frac{1}{\sigma^2}\left(E[Z \mid \mathcal{F}_t^X] - r\right)
\end{aligned}
$$

Where,

| $f_t^*$                                                 | Optimal proportion of wealth invested in the risky asset at time $t$.                                            |
| -------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\sigma^2$                                               | Known variance rate of the risky asset's return process.                                                         |
| $c$                                                      | Prior variance of the unobserved drift $Z$ (for the Gaussian prior case).                                       |
| $X_t$                                                    | Observed return process of the risky asset: $X_t = \int_0^t S_u^{-1} \, dS_u$.                                 |
| $\mu$                                                    | Prior mean of $Z$ (for the Gaussian prior case).                                                                 |
| $r$                                                      | Risk-free rate earned by the bond.                                                                               |
| $E[Z \mid \mathcal{F}_t^X]$                            | Posterior mean of the drift $Z$ given the observation history — the general-prior version of $\hat{Z}_t$.       |
| $\mathcal{F}_t^X$                                       | Observation filtration generated by the return process up to time $t$.                                           |

The first line gives the explicit formula for a Gaussian prior $Z \sim N(\mu, c)$; the second line gives the general-prior version. Both are the **certainty equivalent** of the non-Bayesian Merton fraction $f^* = (\mu - r)/\sigma^2$: the unknown drift is replaced by its posterior mean, and the classical rule is applied unchanged. This is the portfolio-theoretic version of the **Optimal Bayesian Gambling Control** (see [[5.0 - Optimal Bayesian Gambling Control (Browne & Whitt, 1996)]]). The proof extends via the same Martingale arguments — the log-utility objective ensures that estimation and control decouple regardless of the prior specification. The formula connects directly to the standard Merton (1971) result and extends it to the partial-information setting.

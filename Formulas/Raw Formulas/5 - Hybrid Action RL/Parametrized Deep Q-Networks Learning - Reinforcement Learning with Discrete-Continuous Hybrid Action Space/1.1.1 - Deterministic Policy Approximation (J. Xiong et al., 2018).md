The intractable $\sup_{x_k \in X_k}$ in the Hybrid Bellman Equation (see [[1.1 - Hybrid Bellman Equation (J. Xiong et al., 2018)]]) must be approximated for practical computation. The paper introduces a **deterministic policy network** $x_k(\cdot; \theta) : S \to X_k$ that learns to produce the continuous parameter maximizing $Q$ for each discrete action $k$. When the Q-network weights $\omega$ are held fixed, the policy network's task is to find $\theta$ such that its output closely tracks the true $\arg\sup$.

$$
Q(s, k, x_k(s; \theta); \omega) \approx \sup_{x_k \in X_k} Q(s, k, x_k; \omega) \quad \text{for each } k \in [K]
$$

Where,

| $Q(s, k, x_k(s; \theta); \omega)$ | Q-value at state $s$ for discrete action $k$ using the policy network's parameter output, evaluated by the Q-network with weights $\omega$. |
| ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $x_k(s; \theta)$                   | Deterministic policy network: maps state $s$ to the continuous parameter $x_k$ for action $k$, parameterized by weights $\theta$.            |
| $\omega$                            | Weights of the Q-network, held fixed during the policy update.                                                                                |
| $\theta$                            | Weights of the deterministic policy network.                                                                                                  |
| $\sup_{x_k \in X_k}$               | True supremum over the continuous parameter space — the target the policy network approximates.                                               |
| $K$                                 | Number of discrete action types (see [[1.0 - Hybrid Action Space (J. Xiong et al., 2018)]]).                                                 |

The **Deterministic Policy Approximation** is the operational bridge between the Hybrid Bellman Equation and a tractable algorithm. By substituting $x_k(s; \theta)$ for the intractable $\arg\sup$, the hybrid Bellman equation collapses to a form resembling the classical DQN Bellman equation with $A = [K]$. The policy network's quality directly determines how closely P-DQN's $\max_k Q(s, k, x_k(s; \theta); \omega)$ tracks the true optimum $\max_k \sup_{x_k} Q(s, k, x_k; \omega)$. This approximation is validated empirically but carries no formal convergence guarantee — the non-convexity of $Q$ in $x_k$ means gradient ascent on $\theta$ may find local rather than global optima.

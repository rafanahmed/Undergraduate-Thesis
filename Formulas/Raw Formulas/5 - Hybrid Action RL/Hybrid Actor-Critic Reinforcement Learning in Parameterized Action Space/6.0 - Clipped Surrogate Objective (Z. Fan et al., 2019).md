The clipped surrogate objective is the core optimization target of PPO. It constrains policy updates to a trust region by clipping the probability ratio $r_t(\theta)$ between the new and old policies, preventing destructively large steps that could collapse performance. This objective is the template from which the H-PPO discrete and continuous sub-objectives are derived.

$$
L^{\text{CLIP}}(\theta) = \hat{\mathbb{E}}_t\left[\min\left(r_t(\theta)\,\hat{A}_t,\; \text{clip}(r_t(\theta),\, 1 - \epsilon,\, 1 + \epsilon)\,\hat{A}_t\right)\right]
$$

Where,

| $L^{\text{CLIP}}(\theta)$                                   | Clipped surrogate objective to be maximized.                                                                                  |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $r_t(\theta)$                                                | Probability ratio: $r_t(\theta) = \frac{\pi_\theta(a_t \mid s_t)}{\pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}}(a_t \mid s_t)}$.                 |
| $\pi_\theta(a_t \mid s_t)$                                   | New policy's probability of the action taken at timestep $t$.                                                                 |
| $\pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}}(a_t \mid s_t)$                    | Old policy's probability of the same action (from the data collection policy).                                                |
| $\hat{A}_t$                                                  | [[5.0 - Variance-Reduced Advantage Estimator (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]. Advantage estimator at timestep $t$.                   |
| $\epsilon$                                                   | Clipping hyperparameter. Controls the width of the trust region around $r_t = 1$.                                             |
| $\text{clip}(r_t(\theta), 1 - \epsilon, 1 + \epsilon)$      | Clips the ratio to the interval $[1 - \epsilon, 1 + \epsilon]$.                                                              |
| $\hat{\mathbb{E}}_t$                                         | Empirical expectation over timesteps in the collected trajectory batch.                                                       |

The $\min$ operator implements a **pessimistic bound**: when the advantage is positive (the action is good), the objective increases as $r_t$ grows but is capped when $r_t$ exceeds $1 + \epsilon$; when the advantage is negative (the action is bad), the objective's penalty is capped when $r_t$ falls below $1 - \epsilon$. This prevents the optimizer from exploiting large ratio values to make overly aggressive updates. The clipped surrogate objective achieves the stability benefits of TRPO's KL-divergence constraint without the computational cost of second-order optimization. In H-PPO, this generic objective is specialized into two independent versions — one for the discrete policy (see [[6.1 - Discrete Clipped Objective (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]) and one for the continuous policy (see [[6.2 - Continuous Clipped Objective (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]).

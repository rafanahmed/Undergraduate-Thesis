The discrete clipped objective specializes the PPO clipped surrogate (see [[6.0 - Clipped Surrogate Objective (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]) for the discrete sub-actor of the hybrid actor-critic architecture. It uses a probability ratio computed exclusively from the discrete policy $\pi_{\theta_d}$, without conditioning on the continuous policy. The discrete policy is produced by applying softmax to the logit outputs of the discrete actor network, and the advantage estimate is shared with the continuous sub-actor.

$$
L^{\text{CLIP}}_d(\theta_d) = \hat{\mathbb{E}}_t\left[\min\left(r^d_t(\theta_d)\,\hat{A}_t,\; \text{clip}(r^d_t(\theta_d),\, 1 - \epsilon,\, 1 + \epsilon)\,\hat{A}_t\right)\right]
$$

Where,

| $L^{\text{CLIP}}_d(\theta_d)$                       | Discrete clipped surrogate objective for the discrete sub-actor.                                                                |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $r^d_t(\theta_d)$                                    | Discrete probability ratio: $r^d_t(\theta_d) = \frac{\pi_{\theta_d}(a \mid s_t)}{\pi_{\theta_{d(\text{old})}}(a \mid s_t)}$.   |
| $\pi_{\theta_d}(a \mid s_t)$                         | New discrete policy's probability of discrete action $a$ at state $s_t$.                                                       |
| $\pi_{\theta_{d(\text{old})}}(a \mid s_t)$            | Old discrete policy's probability of the same action (from the data collection policy).                                        |
| $\hat{A}_t$                                          | [[5.0 - Variance-Reduced Advantage Estimator (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]. Shared advantage estimator.                              |
| $\epsilon$                                           | Clipping hyperparameter (see [[6.0 - Clipped Surrogate Objective (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]).                                      |
| $\hat{\mathbb{E}}_t$                                 | Empirical expectation over timesteps.                                                                                           |

The probability ratio $r^d_t$ considers **only the discrete policy** — it measures how much more or less likely the new discrete policy makes the action $a$ that was actually selected, compared to the old discrete policy. The continuous parameters $x_a$ do not appear in this objective. Despite this independence, the discrete and continuous actors learn to coordinate through two implicit channels: (1) the shared state encoding network propagates gradient information between both heads, and (2) the shared advantage signal $\hat{A}_t$ ensures both actors are optimized toward the same value criterion. The discrete policy is parameterized as a **softmax** distribution over $k$ logit outputs.

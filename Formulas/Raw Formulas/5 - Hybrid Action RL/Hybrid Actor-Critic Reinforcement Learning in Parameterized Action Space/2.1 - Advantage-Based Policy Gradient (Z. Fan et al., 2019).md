The advantage-based policy gradient refines the stochastic policy gradient (see [[2.0 - Stochastic Policy Gradient (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]) by replacing the action-value function $Q^{\pi_\theta}(s, a)$ with the advantage function $A^{\pi_\theta}(s, a) = Q^{\pi_\theta}(s, a) - V^{\pi_\theta}(s)$. Since the state-value baseline $V^{\pi_\theta}(s)$ is independent of the action, it vanishes under the policy gradient expectation and does not introduce bias, while substantially reducing the variance of the gradient estimator. This formulation is the direct ancestor of the PPO objective used by H-PPO.

$$
\nabla_\theta J(\pi_\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{s,a}\left[\nabla_\theta \log \pi_\theta(a \mid s)\, A^{\pi_\theta}(s, a)\right]
$$

Where,

| $\nabla_\theta J(\pi_\theta)$              | Gradient of the expected policy value with respect to $\theta$.                                                                                                               |
| ------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\nabla_\theta \log \pi_\theta(a \mid s)$   | Score function (see [[2.0 - Stochastic Policy Gradient (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]).                                                                                              |
| $A^{\pi_\theta}(s, a)$                      | Advantage function. $A^{\pi_\theta}(s, a) = Q^{\pi_\theta}(s, a) - V^{\pi_\theta}(s)$. Measures how much better action $a$ is compared to the average action at state $s$.   |
| $Q^{\pi_\theta}(s, a)$                      | Action-value function under policy $\pi_\theta$ (see [[1.0 - Bellman Equation (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]).                                                                       |
| $V^{\pi_\theta}(s)$                         | State-value function. Expected return from state $s$ under policy $\pi_\theta$, averaged over all actions.                                                                    |
| $\mathbb{E}_{s,a}$                          | Expectation over state-action pairs drawn from the policy $\pi_\theta$.                                                                                                       |

The advantage function $A^{\pi_\theta}(s, a)$ is positive when action $a$ is better than average and negative when it is worse, providing a **centered gradient signal** that reduces variance without introducing bias. This formulation is critical to the H-PPO framework: the hybrid actor-critic architecture uses a state-value critic $V(s)$ to compute the advantage estimate $\hat{A}_t$ (see [[5.0 - Variance-Reduced Advantage Estimator (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]), which then appears in the clipped surrogate objectives for both the discrete and continuous sub-actors. The advantage-based formulation justifies the architectural choice of $V(s)$ as the critic — it is precisely the baseline needed to compute the advantage.

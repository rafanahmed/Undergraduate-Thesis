The continuous clipped objective specializes the PPO clipped surrogate (see [[6.0 - Clipped Surrogate Objective (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]) for the continuous sub-actor of the hybrid actor-critic architecture. It uses a probability ratio computed exclusively from the continuous policy $\pi_{\theta_c}$, evaluated at the parameter $x_a$ that was executed for the selected discrete action $a$. The continuous policy is parameterized as a **Gaussian distribution** whose mean and variance are output by the continuous actor network.

$$
L^{\text{CLIP}}_c(\theta_c) = \hat{\mathbb{E}}_t\left[\min\left(r^c_t(\theta_c)\,\hat{A}_t,\; \text{clip}(r^c_t(\theta_c),\, 1 - \epsilon,\, 1 + \epsilon)\,\hat{A}_t\right)\right]
$$

Where,

| $L^{\text{CLIP}}_c(\theta_c)$                       | Continuous clipped surrogate objective for the continuous sub-actor.                                                                |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| $r^c_t(\theta_c)$                                    | Continuous probability ratio: $r^c_t(\theta_c) = \frac{\pi_{\theta_c}(x_a \mid s_t)}{\pi_{\theta_{c(\text{old})}}(x_a \mid s_t)}$. |
| $\pi_{\theta_c}(x_a \mid s_t)$                       | New continuous policy's probability density of parameter $x_a$ at state $s_t$.                                                     |
| $\pi_{\theta_{c(\text{old})}}(x_a \mid s_t)$          | Old continuous policy's density of the same parameter (from the data collection policy).                                          |
| $x_a$                                                | Continuous parameter actually executed for the selected discrete action $a$ (see [[4.0 - Parameterized Action Space (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]). |
| $\hat{A}_t$                                          | [[5.0 - Variance-Reduced Advantage Estimator (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]. Shared advantage estimator.                                 |
| $\epsilon$                                           | Clipping hyperparameter (see [[6.0 - Clipped Surrogate Objective (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]).                                         |
| $\hat{\mathbb{E}}_t$                                 | Empirical expectation over timesteps.                                                                                              |

The continuous probability ratio $r^c_t$ considers **only the continuous policy** — it measures the density ratio for the parameter vector $x_a$ that was actually used with the selected action $a$. The discrete action choice does not appear in this objective. Combined with the Discrete Clipped Objective (see [[6.1 - Discrete Clipped Objective (Z. Fan et al., 2019)]]), these two objectives constitute the complete H-PPO training signal. The two policies $\pi_{\theta_d}$ and $\pi_{\theta_c}$ are treated as **independent distributions** during optimization, not as a joint distribution. This decoupling avoids the joint-learning instability reported by Wei et al. (2018) in their hierarchical approach, where conditioning the parameter policy on the discrete action policy led to training instability.
